About

My name is Cristina, and I work at EcoQuants, where I manage, prepare, visualize, and conduct analyses with environmental data.

While at EcoQuants, I have contributed to a variety of projects and dashboards, including the Marine Biodiversity Observation Network (MBON) Pole-to-Pole Project and the U.S. Department of Energy Marine Energy Toolkit. I have also conducted some of my own personal projects, including a Shiny app visualizing intertidal temperature trends in the Santa Barbara Channel of California and a Shiny app displaying disparities in food security across various factors in the U.S.

I strive to balance my creative interests with technical expertise and ethical concerns, and thus am fascinated by the power of data science and computer programming as tools to achieve environmental goals. In the future, I hope to continue to deepen my data science skills for the purpose of comprehending, communicating, and developing solutions to complex environmental problems.

When I’m not coding or working on environmental projects, I like to hike, play guitar, and listen to music.
